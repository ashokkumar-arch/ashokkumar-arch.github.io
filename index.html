Background

About

A rare combination of kernel-level Linux engineering, VMware platform consulting, and active cloud-native architecture work.

Began with enterprise Linux at Red Hat, working in the kernel support team — diagnosing low-level RHEL issues, supporting complex enterprise environments, and building the systems-level foundation that informs every architectural decision made today.

At VMware (Broadcom), was deployed directly to a government banking client on day one — no probationary ramp — and performed the full consulting motion: stakeholder mapping from zero, application requirements elicitation, gap analysis of existing Tanzu environments, risk identification, and benefits realization toward platform modernization.

Currently building a hands-on architecture portfolio covering the full spectrum: physical data center fabric through OpenShift on VCF+NSX, complete VM migration to OpenShift Virtualization with full NSX decommission, hybrid cloud connectivity with ROSA on AWS, and standalone AWS cloud engineering projects.

The portfolio environment — NovaCrestBank, a fictional government banking institution — is deliberately designed to reflect real enterprise constraints: PCI-DSS v4.0 and NIST SP 800-53 compliance requirements, HSM-backed PKI, dual-fabric FC SAN, Thales Luna HSM integration, CyberArk PAM, and a complete Cisco leaf-spine physical fabric.

Every guide in this portfolio documents not just the how, but the why — the decision rationale, the trade-offs considered, the alternatives rejected. This is the thinking that separates a consulting architect from a configuration engineer.

"The environment was a government bank. Workloads had been running on VMs. App modernization was just starting. On day one at the client site, the objective was to bring architectural clarity, stakeholder alignment, and delivery momentum — without a playbook."

Stakeholder Engagement & Advisory
Deployed directly to client site on day one with no probationary ramp — mapped the entire stakeholder landscape from zero, from HR through C-level executives to L1 operations engineers, identifying influence, interest, and communication preferences for each.
Facilitated cross-functional meetings between application owners, operations team leads (networking, security), and vendor sales teams — maintaining a self-managed stakeholder register and engagement plan throughout.
Conducted individual requirements elicitation sessions with each application owner, documenting functional requirements, migration expectations, scheduling constraints, and integration dependencies for every workload targeted for Tanzu migration.
Technical Architecture & Risk Management
Proactively evaluated existing Tanzu cluster architectures and identified security context gaps — recommending ReadOnlyRootFilesystem, runAsUser, fsGroups, capabilities constraints, and network policies aligned with the principle of least privilege.
Identified IP addressing constraints in the existing network plan — only two additional clusters could be provisioned with available pools — and presented a risk register entry with remediation options to relevant stakeholders before the limitation became a delivery blocker.
Resolved an escalated application owner incident (OOM-killed Java application) by diagnosing container memory limit misconfiguration via logs, explaining the OOM-killer mechanism to the development team, and guiding corrective action — converting a confrontational situation into a collaborative one.
Contributed to benefits realization planning by identifying operational pain points (multi-cluster management overhead) as positive risk opportunities — directly supporting the case for ACM adoption and expanding the platform footprint.
Performed the full consulting motion — stakeholder management, communication management, business analysis, risk management, gap analysis, and platform advocacy — in a live government banking environment, without a formal project management mandate.
