2. What is Disaster Recovery (DR)

2.1 Beginner-Friendly Explanation

Disaster Recovery means restoring IT services after a failure or disaster.

2.2 Real Enterprise Understanding

Failure Scenario Enterprise Recovery Goal
Single VM failure Automatic HA recovery
ESXi host failure vSphere HA restart
Cluster failure Cluster restore
Storage corruption Snapshot recovery
Site outage Secondary site failover

3. Difference Between Backup and Disaster Recovery

Backup Disaster Recovery
Protects data Protects business operations
Restores files Restores applications and infrastructure
Usually slower Designed for rapid recovery
Focuses on retention Focuses on availability

4. What is Business Continuity (BC)

4.1 Reader Doubt Clarification

Question:

Is disaster recovery enough?

Why do enterprises separately discuss business continuity?

4.2 Real Enterprise Explanation

Disaster Recovery restores systems.

Business Continuity ensures the business keeps operating during disasters.

Example:

  • Banking transactions continue
  • ATM operations continue
  • Customer web portals remain online
  • Internal payment systems remain available

13. Enterprise Cross-Site Replication

13.1 Real Enterprise Requirement

Enterprises never keep backups only inside the primary data center.

Critical Enterprise Principle:

If backups remain only in the primary site:

• Ransomware may encrypt backups
• Storage failures may destroy backups
• Fire/flood/power events may destroy everything

13.2 Enterprise DR Strategy

Backup Copy Location
Primary Backup Mumbai DC
Secondary Backup Hyderabad DR Site
Immutable Backup Offline object storage

18. Enterprise etcd Protection

18.1 Why etcd Matters

etcd is the Kubernetes control plane database.

Critical Understanding:

If etcd becomes corrupted:

• Kubernetes API may fail
• Cluster state may be lost
• Workloads may become unrecoverable
