1. Overview

This guide installs VMware ESXi on the enterprise physical infrastructure prepared in the previous guide.

These ESXi hosts become the foundational compute layer for:

  • VMware Cloud Foundation (VCF)
  • VMware NSX
  • vSAN
  • Tanzu Kubernetes Grid Service (VKS)
  • Enterprise Kubernetes platforms
  • AI-ready future workloads
Important:

Many people think VMware starts at vCenter.

No.

Everything starts from ESXi.

If ESXi installation, networking, DNS, storage, or time synchronization are incorrect, the entire VMware Cloud Foundation deployment becomes unstable.

2. Why Enterprises Use ESXi

A reader may get a question:

Question:

If Kubernetes exists, then why do enterprises still use ESXi and VMware infrastructure?

Why not directly deploy Kubernetes on bare metal?

2.1 Real Enterprise Reasoning

In real-world enterprise environments:

  • Enterprises already run thousands of virtual machines
  • Security and governance policies already exist around VMware
  • Backup and DR tooling already integrates with VMware
  • Infrastructure teams already know VMware operations
  • VMware provides enterprise-grade HA, DRS, and lifecycle management

Tanzu extends Kubernetes into this already-existing enterprise operational model instead of replacing everything completely.

2.2 Why This Matters for Tanzu

VMware Tanzu is designed specifically for enterprises that already run VMware infrastructure.

Tanzu allows:

  • VMware administrators to operate Kubernetes infrastructure
  • Platform teams to provide Kubernetes as a service
  • Developers to consume Kubernetes without managing infrastructure
  • Enterprises to modernize gradually instead of replacing everything

Important:

A reader may get a question:

Why use HPE custom ESXi image instead of standard VMware ISO?

Real-world answer:

Enterprise servers require vendor-specific drivers and firmware integrations.

Using the vendor-customized image reduces:

• Driver mismatch issues
• NIC compatibility problems
• Storage controller failures
• vSAN instability

7.2 Why Static IPs?

A reader may get a question:

Question:

Why not use DHCP for ESXi management interfaces?

Real-world answer:

  • Infrastructure components require predictable addressing
  • VCF automation depends on stable management IPs
  • DNS registration consistency is required
  • Certificates depend on stable FQDN mappings
