1. Overview

This guide defines the enterprise naming conventions and object-standardization strategy used throughout the NovaCrestBank VMware Cloud Foundation, NSX, and Tanzu Kubernetes Service platform.

Consistent naming standards are extremely important in real-world enterprise environments because naming consistency directly affects:

  • Operational troubleshooting
  • Automation reliability
  • Monitoring and observability
  • Disaster recovery operations
  • Infrastructure-as-Code consistency
  • Platform engineering workflows
  • Security policy management
  • Enterprise governance
Important:

In enterprise environments, inconsistent naming standards become operational problems at scale. The purpose of this guide is to establish predictable, repeatable, automation-friendly naming conventions across the entire platform stack.

2. Enterprise Naming Principles

2.1 Standardization Goals

Goal Description
Consistency Use predictable naming structures across all environments.
Readability Names should immediately communicate purpose and location.
Automation Friendly Names must work cleanly with Terraform, Ansible, GitOps, and CI/CD pipelines.
Scalability Naming structures must support future platform expansion.
Disaster Recovery Alignment Primary and DR site naming must remain consistent.

2.2 General Naming Rules

  • Use lowercase characters only
  • Use hyphens instead of spaces
  • Avoid underscores where possible
  • Names should reflect environment and purpose
  • Keep naming structures automation-friendly
  • Avoid ambiguous abbreviations

4. DNS and Domain Naming Standards

4.1 Enterprise Internal Domain

The enterprise platform uses: 

novacrestbank.internal

instead of legacy .local naming conventions.

Why .internal Instead of .local?

Modern enterprise environments avoid .local because of multicast DNS (mDNS) conflicts, certificate complications, and legacy interoperability issues.

Using:

novacrestbank.internal

provides cleaner enterprise namespace separation and avoids public DNS overlap risks.

10. Disaster Recovery Naming Standards

10.1 DR Consistency Principle

Disaster recovery environments should mirror primary production naming structures wherever possible.

This improves:

  • Operational familiarity
  • Automation consistency
  • Failover predictability
  • Troubleshooting simplicity

10.2 DR Naming Examples

Primary DR Equivalent
