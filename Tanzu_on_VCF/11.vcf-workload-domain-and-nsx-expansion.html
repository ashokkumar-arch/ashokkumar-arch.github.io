1. Overview

In the previous guides, we deployed the VMware Cloud Foundation management domain, NSX foundation services, enterprise PKI, and enterprise identity architecture.

However, a reader may immediately get an important question:

Question:

If the management domain already exists:

• Why do we need workload domains?
• Why not run Tanzu clusters directly inside the management domain?
• Why do enterprises separate workloads?
• What actually happens in real-world enterprise production environments?

1.1 Real Enterprise Explanation

In real enterprise environments, management infrastructure and production workloads are separated.

The management domain is reserved for:

  • vCenter
  • SDDC Manager
  • NSX Managers
  • Aria Suite
  • Infrastructure services

Production workloads are deployed into dedicated workload domains.

2. What is a Workload Domain?

2.1 Simple Understanding

A workload domain is an isolated vSphere + NSX environment managed by VMware Cloud Foundation.

4. Why Enterprises Separate Workload Domains

4.1 Real Production Scenario

Imagine a production Kubernetes cluster experiences:

  • Memory exhaustion
  • Storage latency
  • Kernel panic
  • GPU driver crash
  • Massive pod scheduling failures

If Kubernetes workloads share the same management infrastructure cluster:

Critical Risk:

The entire private cloud platform itself becomes unstable.

Meaning:

• vCenter may become unstable
• NSX may become unstable
• SDDC Manager may fail
• Entire infrastructure management may become unavailable

4.2 Real Enterprise Practice

Therefore, enterprises separate:

  • Management infrastructure
  • Production Kubernetes workloads
  • AI/GPU workloads
  • Development environments

6. NSX Expansion Architecture

6.1 Why NSX Expansion is Required

A reader may get another important question:

Question:

NSX already exists in the management domain.

Why expand NSX again?

6.2 Real Enterprise Explanation

The NSX management plane already exists.

However:

  • Additional transport zones are required
  • Additional edge capacity is required
  • Additional overlay networking is required
  • Additional workload-segment networking is required
